SCHOOL PROJECT

As a case study for my Spatial Design course, I have designed a study room that can be temporarily installed in an indoor mezzanine at SFU Surrey campus. I created a prototype using cardboard and a 3D digital model using Rhinoceros 3D to simulate the physical model.

RHINO
HANDCRAFT

Project Summary

The end users of this project were students who may carry their backpacks or things that may be large in volumes. Initially, I drew ideation sketches that had less consideration for human factors or ergonomics. As I iterated the ideation process and prototyping, I found some issues in terms of human dimensions and user experience.

After getting feedback from the instructor and classmates, I referred to the human factors and ergonomics of the 50th percentile to improve the design and the dimension of the study room. Moreover, simulating the movement of circulation using a figurine helped me understand the user experience of using the study booth better.

Process Analysis

Figure 1

To begin the project, I started sketching ideations following the given dimensions of the project. However, as you can see in Figure 1, the design was too basic. Moreover, the human factors were not considered well and the single panel was too close to the structure.

Figure 2

To have more unique design personality and allow better movement circulation, I added multiple vertical panels that work as a partition for users to have more privacy (Figure 2). However,I got feedback from a classmate, saying that the ramp inside the study room was making it inconvenient to enter and it was not adjustable for different users.

Figure 3

Thus, as Figure 3 displays, I made the chair slidable so that users can move the chair and fine their own comfortable position. Also, it works for users who want to stand up while using the table.

Issues

As I built a low-fidelity prototype using cardboard, I found that it was difficult to install the partition with multiple vertical panels, and the entrance was not large enough to fit the users with wheelchairs. Also, in terms of unity, it looked not consistent with the booth as a major element of this project.

Figure 4

Based on the feedback that I got, as a final iteration (Figure 4), I redesigned the partition in a way that it could still keep the user’s privacy safe, but allow more lighting inside and let people outside of the box be able to know if there is a person inside or not. Also, most importantly, I added a ramp at the entrance so that users with wheelchairs have access to the inside of the booth.

Reflection

Through this project, I was able to understand the importance of understanding the human factors and ergonomics to have the right dimension of a prototype. I believe it will save more time as a few iterations can be skipped. Also, the more I am aware of the human factors and ergonomics, the more suitable the structures become. One thing that I will do differently is to create a digital model and a figurine first and simulate the user experience. After I feel confident with the design and the model, I will build a physical model. This way, any modification will be much easier, and I do not waste materials for iterations.
